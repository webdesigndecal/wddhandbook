Introduction

What is design? Well-designed websites and apps are often called "beautiful", but is that all there is to it? As you'll learn, design is much more than aesthetics, and requires understanding of user cognition, behavior, and needs.



Defining Design

Design is really about problem solving. How might we make products, services, etc. that serve their purpose in the most effective way possible? Different kinds of design address different aspects of "effectiveness".



We'll be focusing on the following two types of design:


User Interface (UI) Design involves the space where interactions between the user and machine occur. This might come in the form of a remote control or navigation through a website.



User Experience (UX) Design has to do with a person's emotions and attitudes about using a product, system, or service.



Image source: https://dev.to/mkilmer/an-introduction-to-ux-f75



Some other adjacent forms of design include product design (which is a product-focused approach to UIUX design), graphic design (creating visuals that convey meaning to viewers, and industrial design (the physical appearance, functionality, and fabrication of a product).



Design Process



The process of reaching your final design is incredibly important for creating high quality products. Let's go through the drafting process for desiging a website.



Low Fidelity



Image source: https://medium.com/@robertsmith_co/validating-your-product-design-ideas-with-low-fidelity-wireframes-fba03b84af23

Low fidelity wireframes are quick and dirty visuals of the overall layout of the webpage, illustrating over-arching ideas of placement, spacing, and simple interaction. Low fidelity wireframes can be sketched on paper or created through design software. This stage is best for brainstorming and narrowing down what design to develop.
